Threshold

A slower way of saying yes.

You found this. Maybe Andre sent you, maybe a friend, maybe you went looking. However you arrived, this is the door, and the door opens both ways.

The Living Village is two days and one night, August 15-16, at The Living Centre near London, Ontario. A small group, deliberately. A shared meal. A fire. The land. Each other.

It is not a festival you attend. It is a village you help create.

There are no tickets here. There is a Reciprocity Pool, a shared body of resources, seeded by a grant, filled by everyone who comes. You bring what you have. Time, skills, goods, currency. You name what you would like back. The pool covers what is needed, and what is left flows toward stewarding the land and what we build through this.

If that sounds like work, it is. The work is the point.

What follows is a slower way of saying yes than buying a ticket. Take it at the pace it asks of you.

The Pool

A shared body of resources.

Our current economy treats money and time as the main units of exchange. Nature works differently. Exchange is reciprocal, seasonal, and woven into place.

The Reciprocity Pool was seeded with eight thousand dollars in grant funding. That money does not belong to anyone. It is the village before the village arrives.

Everyone who comes adds to the pool, in their own way. Some bring more money. Some bring more time. Some bring food, or a skill, or a song, or a willingness to sweep the lodge at the end of the night. None of these are ranked.

The pool covers what is needed first. Food, firewood, the people who keep things running. What is left flows toward stewarding the land we are guests on, and toward the next iteration of this work.

Some arrive with full pockets. Some arrive with full hands. Both fill the pool.

The Vow

A small promise to the village.

What you bring is yours to choose. How you show up is the village's to ask.

  • I will fulfill what I take on, even if the village asks me to swap roles.
  • I understand that what I add to the pool is a gift to the people beside me, not a transaction.
